OVERVIEW
────────
The system uses a 5-layer pipeline to move from raw questionnaire
answers → a scientifically optimised, personalised portfolio.
Each layer adds more intelligence than a simple score-and-filter approach.

LAYER 1 — RISK SCORING (The Foundation)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

HOW IT WORKS:
The client answers 24 questions. Each answer maps to a numeric score.
All scores are summed and divided by the maximum possible (30 points).

KEY VARIABLES USED:
  • knowledge_level     — investment experience (0–3 pts)
  • drop_reaction       — what they would do in a 20% crash (0–3 pts)
  • risk_relationship   — psychological relationship with risk (0–3 pts)
  • loss_vs_gain        — which outcome upsets them more (0–1 pts)
  • max_loss            — maximum annual loss tolerated (0–3 pts)
  • income_loss_runway  — how long they could survive without income (0–4 pts)
  • debt_situation      — current debt level (0–3 pts)
  • withdrawal_time     — how much they expect to withdraw in 2 years (0–3 pts)
  • invest_style        — passive vs. active preference (0–4 pts)
  • market_adjustment   — willingness to adjust to market conditions (0–3 pts)

SCORING:
  Score < 35% of max  →  Conservative (capital preservation focus)
  Score 35%–65%       →  Moderate     (balanced growth and income)
  Score > 65%         →  Aggressive   (maximum long-term growth)

NEW QUESTIONS ADDED (from Design Doc):
  • time_horizon       — how many years before funds are needed
  • target_amount      — specific dollar target (feeds Monte Carlo)
  • risk_comfort       — drawdown comfort on a 1–10 scale
  • sector_view        — specific sector beliefs (feeds Black-Litterman)
  • performance_benchmark — what they compare their portfolio to
  • usd_liabilities    — USD-denominated debts
  • inflation_protection — preference for inflation hedging
  • loss_vs_gain       — loss aversion detection
  • market_adjustment  — willingness to rebalance

LAYER 2 — BEHAVIORAL RISK ADJUSTMENT (Psychology Layer)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

WHY IT EXISTS:
Research in behavioural finance shows that what people SAY they will do
in a market crash and what they ACTUALLY do are often very different.
This layer detects those gaps and adjusts the profile accordingly.

BIASES DETECTED:
  1. Loss Aversion Gap
     VARIABLES: drop_reaction + loss_vs_gain + max_loss
     SIGNAL: Client says they'd sell if market drops, fears losses more
             than missing gains, AND has low stated tolerance.
     ACTION: Profile moved one step more conservative.

  2. Overconfidence
     VARIABLES: knowledge_level + income_loss_runway + debt_situation
     SIGNAL: High stated experience but weak financial safety net
             while claiming Aggressive profile.
     ACTION: Profile moderated — protects against forced selling
             during a downturn when cash runs out.

  3. Anchoring Bias
     VARIABLES: performance_benchmark = "The amount I originally invested"
     SIGNAL: Client fixates on their original investment as the
             benchmark — classic loss-aversion anchoring.
     ACTION: Portfolio built with a stronger floor on downside.

  4. Overoptimism
     VARIABLES: max_loss + drop_reaction + income_loss_runway + debt_situation
     SIGNAL: Stated tolerance is extreme (>40% loss is "fine") but
             the client has no safety net. Real-world constraints
             would force an early exit.
     ACTION: Slight conservative shift.

RESULT: A "behavioral_profile" that may differ from the raw "profile".
Both are stored in the report so the client can see the adjustment.


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
LAYER 3 — CORRELATION-AWARE ASSET SELECTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

WHY IT EXISTS:
A simple "pick top-10 assets by score" approach would likely select
assets that all move together — providing no real diversification.
This layer uses the ACTUAL correlation matrix from Dimension Depths.

HOW IT WORKS:
  1. Each candidate asset is scored individually (Layer 1 logic).
  2. Assets are selected one-at-a-time using a greedy algorithm.
  3. At each step: Combined Score = Asset Score − (0.8 × Avg Correlation
     with already-selected assets × 2.0)
  4. This penalises assets that are highly correlated with what's
     already in the portfolio — even if they score well individually.

VARIABLES USED:
  • correlation matrix   — from /api/soc/correlations/assets/ endpoint
  • ticker pairings      — compared across all selected pairs
  • _corr_penalty        — stored per asset, shown in the PDF report as
                           "Diversification Contribution" (High/Medium/Low)

RESULT: A portfolio where assets genuinely move differently from each
other, reducing overall portfolio volatility without sacrificing return.

LAYER 4 — MVO + DYNAMIC WEIGHT ALLOCATION (The Optimisation Engine)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

WHY IT EXISTS:
Fixed percentage allocations (e.g. "always 30% bonds") ignore the
actual mathematical relationship between assets. Mean-Variance
Optimisation (MVO) finds the MATHEMATICALLY OPTIMAL weights.

STRATEGY SELECTION:
  Aggressive + Growth goal    →  Maximise Sharpe Ratio
  Conservative                →  Minimise Volatility (Global Min Variance)
  Income / Capital Preserve   →  Constrained MVO (yield ≥ 3.5%)

FORMULA — MAXIMUM SHARPE RATIO:
  Maximise: (Portfolio Return − Risk-Free Rate) / Portfolio Volatility
  Where Risk-Free Rate = 4% (approx. JMD T-Bill yield)

FORMULA — MINIMUM VOLATILITY:
  Minimise: √(wᵀ Σ w)
  Subject to: Σw = 1, 0.02 ≤ wᵢ ≤ 0.40

FORMULA — INCOME CONSTRAINED:
  Minimise: √(wᵀ Σ w)
  Subject to: Σw = 1, Σ(wᵢ × yieldᵢ) ≥ 3.5%

INPUTS:
  • mu (μ)      — expected return vector from total_expected_return
  • Sigma (Σ)   — covariance matrix from /api/soc/covariance/assets/
  • yields      — income_yield_ann per asset (income strategy only)

FALLBACK:
  If scipy is unavailable or optimisation fails, the system uses
  a score-proportional softmax allocation with dynamic class caps
  driven by the client context (goal_equity_bias, resilience_score,
  usd_bias, inflation_hedge_need, etc.)

LAYER 4b — MONTE CARLO SIMULATION (Goal-Based Validation)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

WHY IT EXISTS:
MVO tells us the OPTIMAL portfolio for today's expected conditions.
Monte Carlo asks: "But what if things don't go perfectly?"

HOW IT WORKS:
  1. Take the MVO-optimised weights (w).
  2. Calculate portfolio expected return (p_ret) and volatility (p_vol).
  3. Simulate 5,000 random annual return paths over 10 years using
     a normal distribution: returns ~ N(p_ret, p_vol)
  4. For each simulation, compound the returns: Final Wealth = $100,000
     × ∏(1 + annual_return)
  5. Analyse the 5,000 final wealth values.

VARIABLES CALCULATED:
  • prob_double     — probability of doubling the initial investment
  • prob_preserve   — probability of keeping at least 90% of capital
  • median_final    — median outcome across all simulations
  • p10_final       — worst-case 10th percentile outcome
  • p90_final       — best-case 90th percentile outcome

FEEDBACK LOOP (per Design Doc "Client Conversation" logic):
  If prob_double < 30%: the advisory note flags this, suggesting the
  client may need to increase savings or accept a moderate profile.

FUTURE ENHANCEMENT:
  Replace normal distribution with skewed/fat-tailed distributions
  using skewness and excess_kurtosis from asset data — particularly
  important for Jamaican assets which can have sudden large price moves.

LAYER 5 — CONFIDENCE SCORE (Quality Assurance)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The confidence score (0–100) gives the client a transparent view of
how well-matched their portfolio is to their profile.

COMPONENTS:
  Questionnaire completeness        20 pts  (# of key fields answered)
  Profile-allocation alignment      25 pts  (actual weights vs. ideal)
  Diversification quality           25 pts  (1 − avg pairwise correlation)
  Financial resilience              15 pts  (runway + debt situation)
  Absence of behavioral flags       15 pts  (−5 per detected bias)

GRADE:
  80–100: A  Excellent match
  65–79:  B  Good match
  50–64:  C  Moderate match
  <50:    D  Review recommended

DATA SOURCES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

All asset variables come from the Dimension Depths API:
  /api/soc/assets/                  — returns, volatility, yield, class
  /api/soc/covariance/assets/       — Σ matrix for MVO
  /api/soc/correlations/assets/     — correlation matrix for diversification
  /api/soc/correlations/subclasses/ — super-class correlation structure

VARIABLES USED FROM EACH ASSET:
  total_expected_return   →  MVO expected return vector (μ)
  volatility_ann          →  fallback covariance estimation
  income_yield_ann        →  income-constrained MVO yield constraint
  sharpe_ratio            →  individual asset scoring bonus
  super_class / asset_class → classification (Cash, Equity, Fixed Income…)
  currency                →  USD bias matching
  semi_deviation_ann      →  (future) downside risk adjustment
  skewness / excess_kurtosis → (future) fat-tail Monte Carlo
